I am a systems and synthetic biologist passionate about combining experimental manipulation and theoretical modelling to better understand and engineer living organisms. My current PhD research is on the ultra-fast growing bacterial chassis Vibrio natriegens: how does its physiology give rise to its exceptional growth? and how will it affect the outcome of engineering interventions? Beyond academic curiosity I am motivated by the enormous potential of reliable 'biology by design' to solve real-world problems.

I plan to use this site to post thoughts on systems and synthetic biology related topics.
